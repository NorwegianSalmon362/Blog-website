First post: wi-fi can kind of see through walls expand

29 June 2026

this started from a reddit post and a youtube video about CSI, which is apparently a way of reading how wi-fi signals change when people move around.

i found this really really cool post about using wi-fi signals to detect people through walls and stuff. the thing is called CSI, which is channel state information, and the rough idea is that your router is already sending radio waves around at about 5 ghz all the time.

those waves bounce off basically everything, including your body. when someone moves, the signal changes in these tiny specific ways, and those changes can say a lot about what moved and how it moved. it feels kind of insane that normal wi-fi can hold that much information in the noise.

i do not know how to turn the CSI data into actual 3d images yet, or if that is even the right way to think about it, but that might be something worth digging into. maybe the interesting part is not making a clean picture, but learning what patterns are hidden in the signal.

tomorrow i want to expand on ai jailbreaking and layered jailbreaks, because that feels like it connects to this same thing: weird hidden systems, weird side channels, and the ways people figure out what they were not supposed to see.
